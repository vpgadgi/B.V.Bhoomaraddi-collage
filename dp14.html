About the Department

The Department was established in the year 1962. Two laboratories are furnished with essential equipment, specimens, lab work materials, and charts. The department provides a certificate course in mushroom cultivation, fruit preservation, and algal cultivation. The botanical garden serves as the repository of source material for botanical studies and conserves and enriches plant bio-diversity.

Objectives of the Department

  1. Enhance the knowledge of students with recent developments in the field of plant science.
  2. Improve skills and competence for global industries.
  3. Save the environment to save life.
  4. Develop a research culture among staff and students.
  5. Train students in a wide range of science-based skills for future prospects.

Future Plan

To prepare the Digital flora of The college campus. Apply for more research projects. Ex-situ conservation of some medicinal plants, especially rare and endangered plants of Bidar District. Make the campus plastic-free. Motivate students to be entrepreneurs in the production of Bio-fertilizers like Spirulina, Azolla, Anabeana, Pistia, etc. Start PG course in Botany.
